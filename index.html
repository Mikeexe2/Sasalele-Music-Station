                    How the Stream Works

                    HLS (HTTP Live Streaming) is a technology that divides audio into smaller segments, known as "chunks," and transmits them sequentially over the internet. This allows you to begin listening almost immediately, without having to wait for the entire file to download.

                    You can control the playback of the stream using the video player, just like using a regular audio player. You can pause, play, skip, and adjust the volume using the player's controls.

                    Why Some Links Might Not Work?

                    Sometimes, a link might not work because the stream is temporarily unavailable, or there may be an issue with the streaming service.
